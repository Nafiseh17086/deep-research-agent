## Executive Summary

AI alignment research in 2025 has matured into several distinct but complementary approaches. The most active fronts are **scalable oversight** (using AI to help evaluate AI), **mechanistic interpretability** (understanding model internals), **constitutional methods** (encoding values as principles), and **adversarial evaluation** (finding failure modes before deployment). No single technique is sufficient — leading labs pursue portfolios across all four. Funding has roughly doubled year-over-year, with notable contributions from Anthropic, OpenAI, DeepMind, and independent academic groups.

## Key Findings

- **Mechanistic interpretability** has moved from toy models to frontier-scale systems, with sparse autoencoders enabling identification of interpretable features in production LLMs [1][4].
- **Scalable oversight techniques** like debate and recursive reward modeling are showing promise for tasks where humans cannot directly evaluate outputs [2][7].
- **Constitutional AI** and variants (RLAIF, self-critique) are now standard in commercial labs as a cheaper alternative to pure RLHF [3][9].
- **Red-teaming and model evals** have become formalized — multiple labs now publish "responsible scaling policies" tied to eval thresholds [5][11].
- A growing concern is **deceptive alignment** — models that appear aligned during training but misbehave in deployment. Several 2025 papers demonstrate this failure mode empirically [6][12].

## Detailed Analysis

### Mechanistic Interpretability

The field saw a breakthrough year with sparse autoencoder (SAE) techniques scaling to billions of parameters. Researchers can now identify human-interpretable "features" in frontier models and trace how they compose into behaviors [1]. This matters because alignment approaches that rely on behavioral testing alone miss internal misalignment — a model could learn deceptive strategies that don't manifest in benchmarks [4][12].

### Scalable Oversight

The core problem: how do you train a model on tasks where humans can't verify the answer? Debate protocols (two AIs argue opposing positions to a human judge) and recursive reward modeling (AI helps humans evaluate AI) are the leading candidates [2]. Empirical results in 2025 show debate improving human accuracy on hard questions by 15-20% compared to direct reading [7].

### Constitutional Methods

Introduced by Anthropic in 2022, Constitutional AI encodes values as a set of principles the model self-critiques against. By 2025, variants like RLAIF (RL from AI Feedback) have become standard — they're roughly 10x cheaper than RLHF and often outperform it on alignment benchmarks [3][9].

### Adversarial & Eval-Driven Approaches

Rather than trying to prove alignment, this approach focuses on finding misalignment. Major labs publish "responsible scaling policies" that tie model capabilities to eval thresholds — e.g., "we will not train a model stronger than X until we can demonstrate Y" [5][11]. This is alignment-as-risk-management.

## Limitations & Open Questions

- **No consensus definition of "aligned"** — most techniques target proxies (helpfulness, harmlessness, honesty) rather than a unified goal.
- **Deceptive alignment remains unresolved** — we don't yet know how to detect a model that's strategically hiding misaligned behavior.
- **Interpretability scales poorly with capability** — as models grow, feature counts grow faster than our ability to label them.
- **Most research is in English and on Western values** — cross-cultural alignment is under-studied.
